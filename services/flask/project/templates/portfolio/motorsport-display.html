  This product has been made for the customer: KMS

For my graduation internship, I found an assignment at van Kronenburg Motormanagement Systems in Geldrop, Netherlands. They had a working prototype of a digital motorsport display. This product was in a really early stage of development when I started my assignment. The goal of the assignment was to make the product ready for production. To achieve this goal, I had to work on a lot of different subjects, in quite a few different disciplines.





Some of the product specifications are as follows:

  • The user is able to display CAN data from their ECU on a 'Check Engine Page' and a 'Race Page'.
  • The product has 3 buttons to navigate through the menu (3 more are delivered to mount in the vehicle).
  • There are 16 configurable LEDs at the sides of the screen (2 sets of 8), so the user can receive warnings when engine data crosses a configured threshold.
  • There are 16 RPM LEDs which turn on/off when a certain engine RPM has been passed, to show the user when to switch gears.
  • The product contains a GPS module, to be used with a lap-timer functionality. The end user is able to time how fast they have driven over any track in the EU!
  • The product contains an ambient light sensor, to detect the brightness of the ambient lighting, and adjusts LED and screen brightness accordingly.




The product is based on a Raspberry Pi Compute Module 4 as the main processor. This full-blown computer has been chosen, since the prototype was already built using a Raspberry Pi, and we wanted to make sure the hardware would be capable for at least 5 years (the project budget was quite limited, so changing such an integral part would have taken to much time). A custom 'carrier' printed circuit board has been designed (by an external company, under my supervision) to connect all the different sensors and actuators to each other and the Raspberry Pi.





The encasing of the product was also designed by me. Colleagues helped out making the product CNC routable. The screen is an 800x400px display, with 1000 nits of brightness. This screen has been specially sourced to make sure it is clearly readable in bright conditions, without glaring. The end product has a glass screen protector over top, to make sure that the screen itself does not get cracked easily.



The development of this digital motorsport display highlights our commitment to innovation and quality. By focusing on user-friendly design, robust hardware, and advanced functionality, we ensure that our products meet the high standards expected by professional rally and circuit drivers. This project exemplifies our ability to deliver customized, high-performance solutions that can enhance the driving experience and provide crucial real-time data. Whether for professional racing teams or enthusiasts, our dedication to excellence in both hardware and software makes us a trusted partner in the motorsport industry.
